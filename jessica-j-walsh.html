Jessica J. Walsh

Jessica J. Walsh, Ph.D.

PI of Walsh-lab-UNC (UNC Chapel Hill, Dept of Pharmacology; Genetic Medicine Building) since July 2021. Spouse and frequent collaborator of Dan-J-Christoffel (PI of Christoffel-lab-UNC).

Background

  • B.A. Neuroscience & Behavior, Columbia College, Columbia University (2008). Undergrad lab: Gerald Fischbach (Dept of Pharmacology). Began in the Fischbach lab as a high-school student.
  • Research Coordinator, Columbia University Dept of Pharmacology (05/2008–08/2008).
  • M.S. Biomedical Sciences, Icahn School of Medicine at Mount Sinai (2010).
  • Research Coordinator, Mt. Sinai Dept of Neuroscience (05/2010–08/2010).
  • Ph.D. Neuroscience, Icahn School of Medicine at Mount Sinai (08/2013). Mentor: Ming-Hu-Han (Han-lab-Mount-Sinai).
  • Postdoctoral Fellow, Stanford University (10/2013–06/2021, ~8 years). Mentor: Robert-C-Malenka (Malenka-lab-Stanford).
  • Assistant Professor of Pharmacology, UNC Chapel Hill (07/2021–present).

Research program

Walsh's program centers on the brain circuits and molecular mechanisms governing motivated behavior, with major emphasis on autism-spectrum-disorder (ASD). She uses a systems-level approach combining well-defined ASD mouse models (e.g., 16p11.2 deletion, Cntnap2 knockout, Fmr1 knockout), in vivo and slice electrophysiology, optogenetics, and pharmacology to map circuit-level mechanisms of sociability and identify novel pharmacological targets (e.g., 5-HT system, 5-HT1b-receptor). She emphasizes a translational arc: define causal circuits → deep-characterize their physiology → target specific receptors that might be therapeutically tractable for ASD social symptoms. The independent UNC program builds on (a) her Han-lab-Mount-Sinai PhD work on midbrain DA / VTA circuitry in stress susceptibility/resilience and (b) her Malenka-lab-Stanford postdoc work on NAc serotonergic prosocial mechanisms and ASD circuit rescue.

Mentorship lineage

Fischbach (undergrad, Columbia) → Ming-Hu-Han (PhD, Mt. Sinai Pharmacology & Systems Therapeutics) → Robert-C-Malenka (postdoc, Stanford Psychiatry & Behavioral Sciences) → PI at UNC Pharmacology.

Method arc

Slice and in vivo electrophysiology + optogenetics + viral pathway tracing across all three career eras. Added 5-HT pharmacology, MDMA, and ASD-model behavior at Stanford. Now extends at UNC into ASD circuit dissection (Cntnap2, 16p11.2) and 5-HT1B-targeted therapeutics.
